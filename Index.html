How this works: A mathematical optimization model (network simplex algorithm) finds the cheapest possible set of shipping routes across a 20-country trade network, using real UN transport cost data (2021–2023) and port connectivity scores. Disruption scenarios stress-test the network by reducing hub capacity, then measuring how much total shipping costs rise. Flow always remains at 100% — goods find a route — but the price changes. Results are from real model runs, not estimates. · Research by Priyanka Gupta, MS Industrial Engineering, UNC Charlotte · Paper under review: Maritime Policy & Management
