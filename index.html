Key finding from your model: Singapore is the most critical hub — a 30% capacity shock raises total network costs by 36.0%, and a 50% shock doubles costs (+100.0%). The response is near-quadratic, a signature of hub dependency. China shows moderate sensitivity (+17.7% at 50%). Netherlands is near-negligible (+2.2% at 50%), meaning your optimizer finds cost-equivalent reroutes trivially. The Red Sea route cost shock produced effectively zero cost change — your network simplex solution absorbs corridor cost increases by rerouting at no extra cost.
